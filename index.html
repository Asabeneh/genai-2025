Module 1 — Introduction to Generative AI

By the end of this module, learners should be able to explain what Generative AI is, understand how models like GPT and diffusion models work, and identify popular Generative AI tools.

Explanation

Generative Artificial Intelligence refers to a class of AI systems capable of creating new content—such as text, code, music, or images—based on what they have learned from vast datasets.

Traditional AI systems analyze or classify data, while Generative AI produces new outputs.

How it works
  • Transformers — the architecture behind GPT, Claude, Gemini, and Llama.
  • Diffusion models — used in image generation tools like DALL·E, Midjourney, Stable Diffusion.
  • GANs — two networks compete to create realistic outputs.
Real-world analogy

Think of Generative AI as an advanced autocomplete system that can complete full essays, images, or code snippets.

Examples
  • ChatGPT writing a professional email
  • DALL·E generating a logo
  • Copilot suggesting blocks of code

Exercises

  1. Explain the difference between traditional AI and Generative AI in your own words.
  2. Research and list 3 Generative AI tools and describe their purpose.
  3. Use ChatGPT to generate a motivational quote and rewrite it in a funny tone.

Quiz

  1. What is the main goal of Generative AI? Answer: Creation
  2. Which model is used for image generation? Answer: Diffusion
  3. True or False: GANs involve two neural networks competing. True
